Intel Sustainability Story

Sustainability Through the Ages

Intel is on a mission to build a more sustainable future. Discover the milestones and goals that have shaped the company's journey toward cleaner energy, smarter manufacturing, and a better planet.

Solutions, progress, and purpose over time

This timeline highlights Intel’s sustainability goals, from early innovation to today’s net-zero ambition. Hover over each card to learn more and explore the journey on mobile or desktop.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore renamed NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, starting a new era of innovation and laying the groundwork for future sustainability efforts.

Why it matters

This founding moment established the long-term vision for technology that grows smarter, more efficient, and more sustainable over time.

1971

First Microprocessor

The Intel 4004 debuted as the world’s first commercial microprocessor, proving that smaller, smarter technology can help build a more efficient future.

Future impact

Early microprocessor innovation paved the way for energy-efficient devices and more intelligent systems across every industry.

1978

8086 Processor

The launch of the 8086 processor established the x86 architecture, powering countless devices and enabling future improvements in energy-efficient computing.

Why it matters

Platform standardization helped drive technology adoption and gave Intel the scale to invest in sustainable manufacturing practices.

1985

386 Processor

The 386 brought 32-bit architecture to personal computing, demonstrating how advancements in processing power can be paired with more efficient design.

Performance gains

Better performance meant more useful computing for fewer resources, a key principle of sustainable technology progress.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marked Intel’s highest operational greenhouse gas emissions. The company responded by investing in renewable energy, chemical abatement, and efficiency programs.

Turning point

The peak in emissions led Intel to accelerate sustainability initiatives and make long-term climate commitments.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launched the RISE strategy with 2030 goals focused on climate action, water stewardship, waste reduction, and inclusive innovation across the industry.

RISE forward

The RISE strategy anchored Intel’s sustainability work with measurable goals that guide every part of the business.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel committed to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental progress.

Bold ambition

Net-zero by 2040 sets a clear path for continual reduction of Intel’s carbon footprint and a stronger environmental legacy.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieved 99% renewable electricity worldwide, significantly lowering carbon emissions and moving closer to its long-term sustainability goals.

Clean energy

Almost all operations now run on renewable electricity, showing how energy choices can transform a global business.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosted a global Sustainability Summit, bringing together suppliers, governments, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

Shared progress

Collaboration across the supply chain is key to making semiconductor manufacturing greener at scale.
